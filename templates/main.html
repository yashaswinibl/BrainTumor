Brain Tumor

A mass of abnormal cells in the brain. This causes frequent and severe headaches, nausea, vision problems, gradual loss of sensation, hearing problems, and seizures.

Causes:

Tumors develop due to uncontrolled growth of cells in the brain. It can also be caused due to cancer in other parts of the body which spreads to the brain.

Symptoms:

Headache, vision problems and gradual loss of sensation are the commonly observed symptoms.

Diagnosis:

Diagnosis involves physical examination, certain tests and procedures.

Different Types Of Tumors

Gliomas

Gliomas are tumors that arise from glial cells in the brain. They can be either benign or malignant and can cause various neurological symptoms depending on their location and size.

Meningiomas

Meningiomas are typically slow-growing tumors that originate from the meninges, the protective membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. They are usually benign and may not cause symptoms unless they grow large enough to compress nearby structures.

Pituitary

Pituitary tumors are abnormal growths that develop in the pituitary gland, a small gland located at the base of the brain. These tumors can cause hormonal imbalances and various symptoms depending on their size and hormone-secreting activity.
